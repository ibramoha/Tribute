Eng. Kitaw Ejigu

The pride of bonga

Kitaw Ejigu was one of Ethiopia's first aerospace scientists. He also worked for Rockwell International and Boeing.

Here's a time line of Eng. kitaw's life:

  • 1948 -Born in Bonga, Kaffa, Ethiopia
  • 1966 - Graduated as the top student in his class from BahirDar Polytechnic Institute
  • 1967 -Started working at the Ethiopian Automotive Services and Sales Company (EASSCO) as chief technical advisor and assistant manager
  • 1972 - Won a scholarship from the Japanese Overseas Technical Association and traveled to Japan. There, Kitaw studied automotive engineering at Hiroshima University
  • 1976 - moved to the United States, where he began intensive research and training and.
  • 1977 - hired by the Jet Propulsion Lab (JPL) of California Institute of Technology, a NASA research center, in Pasadena, California, where he eventually became the ‘Chief Spacecraft Systems Design Engineer’.
  • 1979 - earned an MBA in 1979.
  • 1981 - Completed his doctorate in space vehicle systems engineering from Northrop University in California.
  • 1982 -Started working for NASA as a system engineer and space research scientist. He collaborated with other scientists on the space shuttle and other rocketry projects..
  • 1990's - Turned his attention to Africa and his homeland Ethiopia hoping to introduce technology based development. He established Trans Tech International, a global technologies service systems which was a privately owned satellite and related systems engineering company. He served as its President/CEO until he passed away.
  • 2001 - visited former Ethiopian university students who took refuge in Kenya after being dismissed from the national university. At their behest, he founded the Ethiopian National United Front (ENUF). He publicly denounced the regime in Ethiopia and its actions and policies. He also played a major role in enrolling some students at the University of Nairobi, and in providing them with some much-needed support.
  • 2002 - Founded a political party, the Ethiopian National United Front, to help overthrow Meles Zenawi 's regime.
  • 2006 - dies at the age of 57.
